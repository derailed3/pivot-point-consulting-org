# Glossary

## Healthcare IT Terms

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **BAA** | Business Associate Agreement. A contract required by HIPAA between a covered entity and a business associate that handles PHI. |
| **Breach Notification Rule** | HIPAA requirement to notify affected individuals, HHS, and media (if large breach) when unsecured PHI is compromised. |
| **Business Associate** | Any entity that creates, receives, maintains, or transmits PHI on behalf of a covered entity. |
| **CMS** | Centers for Medicare and Medicaid Services. Federal agency administering Medicare, Medicaid, and ACA marketplace. |
| **Covered Entity** | A health plan, healthcare clearinghouse, or healthcare provider that transmits health information electronically. |
| **EHR** | Electronic Health Record. A digital version of a patient's medical history maintained by the provider over time. |
| **EMR** | Electronic Medical Record. Digital version of a paper chart used within a single practice. More limited than EHR. |
| **ePHI** | Electronic Protected Health Information. PHI that is created, stored, transmitted, or received electronically. |
| **FHIR** | Fast Healthcare Interoperability Resources. A standard for exchanging healthcare information electronically (HL7). |
| **HIPAA** | Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act. Federal law establishing standards for protecting patient health information. |
| **HITECH** | Health Information Technology for Economic and Clinical Health Act. Strengthens HIPAA enforcement and promotes EHR adoption. |
| **HITRUST CSF** | Health Information Trust Alliance Common Security Framework. A certifiable framework for managing information risk in healthcare. |
| **HL7** | Health Level Seven International. Organization developing standards for exchanging clinical and administrative health data. |
| **Interoperability** | The ability of different health IT systems to exchange, interpret, and use data cooperatively. |
| **Minimum Necessary Standard** | HIPAA principle that only the minimum amount of PHI needed should be used or disclosed. |
| **ONC** | Office of the National Coordinator for Health Information Technology. Federal entity coordinating health IT efforts. |
| **PHI** | Protected Health Information. Any individually identifiable health information held by a covered entity or business associate. |
| **Privacy Rule** | HIPAA regulation establishing standards for the protection of individually identifiable health information. |
| **Security Rule** | HIPAA regulation establishing standards for protecting ePHI through administrative, physical, and technical safeguards. |

| Platform | Description |
|----------|-----------|
| **Epic** | Leading EHR vendor for large health systems. Known for comprehensive functionality and interoperability. |
| **Oracle Health (Cerner)** | Major EHR platform (acquired by Oracle). Strong in government and military healthcare. |
| **MEDITECH** | EHR vendor focused on community hospitals and smaller health systems. |
| **Allscripts** | EHR and practice management solutions for ambulatory and acute care settings. |

## Consulting and Business Terms

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Advisory** | Engagement model providing expert guidance and recommendations without hands-on implementation. |
| **BTV** | Build-Test-Validate. Quality pipeline for documents: build (structure), test (content), validate (brand/compliance). |
| **Diataxis** | Documentation framework organizing content into tutorials, how-to guides, reference, and explanation. |
| **GTM** | Go-to-market. Strategy for launching a product or service to a target market. |
| **PPC** | Pivot Point Consulting (internal abbreviation). Use full name in external-facing content. |
| **Project-Based** | Engagement model with defined scope, deliverables, milestones, and fixed or capped fees. |
| **QBR** | Quarterly Business Review. Executive-level review of practice performance, pipeline, and strategy. |
| **RACI** | Responsible, Accountable, Consulted, Informed. Matrix for defining roles in a process. |
| **RAG** | Red-Amber-Green. Status indicator for health of an engagement or metric. |
| **SOC 2** | Service Organization Control 2. Audit framework for service organizations covering security, availability, processing integrity, confidentiality, and privacy. |
| **SOW** | Statement of Work. Contract defining scope, deliverables, timeline, and fees for an engagement. |
| **Staff Augmentation** | Engagement model providing skilled consultants to supplement a client's existing team. |
| **Sizzle Sheet** | Client-facing one-page consultant profile highlighting key skills and experience. |

## Compliance Certifications

| Certification | Description |
|--------------|-----------|
| **HITRUST CSF** | Comprehensive security framework combining HIPAA, NIST, ISO, and other standards. Requires third-party assessment. |
| **SOC 2 Type I** | Point-in-time assessment of control design against Trust Service Criteria. |
| **SOC 2 Type II** | Assessment of control design AND operating effectiveness over a period (typically 6-12 months). |
