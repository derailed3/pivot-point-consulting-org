# Employee Code of Conduct

## 1. Purpose

The purpose of this Code of Conduct is to establish the standards of professional and ethical behavior expected of all Pivot Point Consulting workforce members. As a healthcare IT consulting firm entrusted with sensitive client data, including Protected Health Information (PHI), Pivot Point Consulting holds its workforce to the highest standards of integrity, professionalism, and compliance.

This Code of Conduct provides guidance on expected behavior, ethical decision-making, and the obligations that come with representing Pivot Point Consulting in all professional interactions. Adherence to this Code is essential to maintaining the trust of our clients, partners, and the communities we serve.

## 2. Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to:

- All Pivot Point Consulting employees, whether full-time, part-time, or temporary.
- All contractors, consultants, and independent professionals engaged by Pivot Point Consulting.
- All interns, fellows, and volunteers.
- Board members and advisory board members, where applicable.

This Code applies to all business activities, whether conducted on Pivot Point Consulting premises, at client sites, at conferences and events, or in remote work environments. It applies to in-person interactions, electronic communications, and social media activity related to Pivot Point Consulting business.

## 4. Professional Conduct

### 4.1 General Standards

All workforce members shall:

- Act with honesty, integrity, and transparency in all professional activities.
- Treat clients, colleagues, vendors, and all stakeholders with dignity and respect.
- Perform their duties with competence, diligence, and professionalism.
- Represent Pivot Point Consulting positively in all professional interactions.
- Maintain current knowledge and skills relevant to their role.
- Honor commitments and meet deadlines.
- Communicate proactively, especially when issues arise that may affect deliverables or timelines.

### 4.2 Client Interactions

When working with clients, workforce members shall:

- Maintain professional boundaries and appropriate relationships.
- Provide honest and accurate assessments and recommendations.
- Not make commitments that Pivot Point Consulting cannot fulfill.
- Respect client policies, procedures, and workplace norms.
- Protect client confidential information with the same rigor as PHI.
- Escalate client concerns through appropriate channels.

### 4.3 Workplace Behavior

Workforce members shall maintain a professional work environment by:

- Being punctual and reliable.
- Participating constructively in meetings and collaborative activities.
- Providing and accepting feedback professionally.
- Resolving conflicts respectfully and through appropriate channels.
- Maintaining a clean and organized workspace, whether in-office or remote.

## 5. Conflicts of Interest

### 5.1 Definition

A conflict of interest arises when a workforce member's personal interests, relationships, or activities interfere with, or have the potential to interfere with, their professional judgment or the best interests of Pivot Point Consulting or its clients.

### 5.2 Disclosure Requirements

Workforce members shall promptly disclose any actual, potential, or perceived conflicts of interest to their manager and to [PLACEHOLDER: compliance or ethics contact]. Disclosures shall be made in writing using [PLACEHOLDER: conflict of interest disclosure form/process].

### 5.3 Examples of Conflicts

Conflicts of interest include, but are not limited to:

- Having a financial interest in a vendor, competitor, or client of Pivot Point Consulting.
- Engaging in outside employment or consulting that conflicts with duties to Pivot Point Consulting.
- Having a personal or family relationship with a client, vendor, or competitor representative that could influence business decisions.
- Using Pivot Point Consulting resources, information, or relationships for personal gain.
- Accepting gifts, entertainment, or favors from clients, vendors, or other third parties that could influence professional judgment.
- Serving on the board of a competitor, client, or vendor without approval.

- Gifts of cash or cash equivalents (gift cards, etc.) are never permitted.
- Business meals and entertainment must be reasonable, infrequent, and have a clear business purpose.
- All gifts received must be reported to [PLACEHOLDER: reporting contact/system].

## 6. Confidentiality and Data Protection

### 6.1 Confidential Information

Workforce members shall protect all confidential information of Pivot Point Consulting, its clients, and its partners. Confidential information includes, but is not limited to:

- Protected Health Information (PHI) and electronic PHI (ePHI).
- Client business information, strategies, and data.
- Pivot Point Consulting proprietary methodologies, tools, and intellectual property.
- Financial information and business plans.
- Employee personal information.
- Security configurations and vulnerability information.
- Contract terms and pricing.

### 6.2 Data Protection Obligations

- Comply with all applicable data protection policies, including the PHI Handling and Data Classification Policy, HIPAA Privacy Rule Compliance Policy, and Information Security Policy.
- Do not access data that is not required for your assigned duties.
- Do not disclose confidential information to unauthorized individuals, including family members and friends.
- Use approved systems and communication channels for all business communications involving confidential data.
- Return all confidential information upon separation from Pivot Point Consulting.

### 6.3 Non-Disclosure

- Workforce members are required to sign a Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA) upon hiring or engagement.
- Confidentiality obligations survive the termination of employment or engagement.
- Unauthorized disclosure of confidential information may result in disciplinary action, termination, and legal action.

## 7. Client Relationships

### 7.1 Client Trust

Pivot Point Consulting's success depends on the trust of our clients. All workforce members shall:

- Act as trusted advisors to clients, providing objective and honest counsel.
- Prioritize client interests while maintaining compliance with laws and regulations.
- Not exploit client relationships for personal gain.
- Maintain appropriate professional boundaries with client personnel.
- Escalate ethical concerns about client requests or practices through appropriate channels.

### 7.2 Client Engagement Standards

- Deliver high-quality work that meets or exceeds client expectations and contractual requirements.
- Communicate transparently about project status, risks, and issues.
- Respect client intellectual property and proprietary information.
- Not solicit client employees during or for [PLACEHOLDER: number] months after an engagement, unless contractually permitted.

### 7.3 Ethical Client Interactions

If a workforce member is asked to do something by a client that conflicts with Pivot Point Consulting policies, applicable laws, or ethical standards, the workforce member shall:

1. Professionally decline the request.
2. Explain the concern to the client if appropriate.
3. Escalate the matter to their Engagement Manager or [PLACEHOLDER: ethics contact].
4. Document the interaction.

## 8. Use of Company Resources

### 8.1 General Standards

Pivot Point Consulting resources, including technology, equipment, supplies, and information, are provided for business use. Workforce members shall:

- Use company resources responsibly and primarily for business purposes.
- Not use company resources for illegal activities, personal business ventures, or activities that conflict with Pivot Point Consulting policies.
- Report damaged, lost, or stolen equipment immediately to [PLACEHOLDER: reporting contact/IT department].
- Return all company property upon separation.

### 8.2 Technology Resources

- Use company-issued devices and software in accordance with the Information Security Policy.
- Do not install unauthorized software on company devices.
- Do not attempt to circumvent security controls.
- Do not use company email or accounts for personal purposes that could be confused with official company communications.

### 8.3 Intellectual Property

- All work product created during employment or engagement with Pivot Point Consulting is the property of Pivot Point Consulting unless otherwise agreed in writing.
- Do not use third-party intellectual property without proper authorization or licensing.
- Report any potential intellectual property infringement to [PLACEHOLDER: legal contact].

## 9. Social Media and Public Communication

### 9.1 Official Communications

- Only authorized spokespersons may make official statements on behalf of Pivot Point Consulting.
- Media inquiries shall be directed to [PLACEHOLDER: communications/PR contact].
- All press releases, published articles, and public presentations about Pivot Point Consulting must be approved by [PLACEHOLDER: approval authority].

### 9.2 Personal Social Media

When using personal social media, workforce members shall:

- Not disclose confidential information, PHI, or proprietary information.
- Clearly distinguish personal opinions from official Pivot Point Consulting positions.
- Not make statements that could be interpreted as representing Pivot Point Consulting without authorization.
- Not disparage Pivot Point Consulting, its clients, partners, or competitors.
- Exercise good judgment and professionalism in all public communications.
- Not reference specific client engagements, client names, or engagement details without written approval.

### 9.3 Professional Social Media (e.g., LinkedIn)

- Workforce members may identify Pivot Point Consulting as their employer on professional social media.
- Thought leadership content related to healthcare IT may be shared, provided it does not contain confidential or proprietary information.
- Client endorsements or case study references require prior written approval from [PLACEHOLDER: approval authority].

## 10. Reporting Violations

### 10.1 Reporting Obligation

All workforce members have an obligation to report known or suspected violations of this Code of Conduct, other Pivot Point Consulting policies, or applicable laws and regulations. Reports should be made to:

- The workforce member's direct manager.
- The Human Resources department ([PLACEHOLDER: contact information]).
- The Compliance/Ethics Hotline ([PLACEHOLDER: hotline number/email]).
- The Privacy Officer or Security Officer (for privacy or security violations).

### 10.2 Whistleblower Protections

Pivot Point Consulting is committed to protecting individuals who report violations in good faith:

- No retaliation, harassment, intimidation, or adverse employment action shall be taken against any workforce member who reports a suspected violation in good faith.
- Retaliation against a reporter is itself a violation of this Code and will result in disciplinary action.
- Reports may be made anonymously through [PLACEHOLDER: anonymous reporting mechanism].
- The identity of reporters shall be kept confidential to the maximum extent possible, consistent with the need to investigate and address the reported concern.

### 10.3 Investigation Process

- All reports of Code of Conduct violations shall be investigated promptly, thoroughly, and impartially.
- Investigations shall be conducted by [PLACEHOLDER: investigation responsibility, e.g., HR, Legal, external investigators].
- Workforce members are required to cooperate fully with investigations.
- Investigation findings and corrective actions shall be documented and retained.

## 11. Anti-Discrimination and Harassment

### 11.1 Equal Opportunity

Pivot Point Consulting is an equal opportunity employer and is committed to providing a workplace free from discrimination. Employment decisions shall be based on qualifications, merit, and business needs, without regard to:

- Race, color, ethnicity, or national origin.
- Sex, gender, gender identity, or gender expression.
- Sexual orientation.
- Religion or creed.
- Age.
- Disability (physical or mental).
- Veteran or military status.
- Pregnancy or parental status.
- Genetic information.
- Any other characteristic protected by applicable federal, state, or local law.

### 11.2 Harassment Prevention

Pivot Point Consulting prohibits all forms of harassment, including:

- **Sexual Harassment**: Unwelcome sexual advances, requests for sexual favors, and other verbal, visual, or physical conduct of a sexual nature.
- **Workplace Harassment**: Conduct based on any protected characteristic that creates an intimidating, hostile, or offensive work environment.
- **Bullying**: Persistent, unwelcome behavior that demeans, intimidates, or humiliates an individual.

Harassment is prohibited whether it occurs in person, electronically, at work locations, client sites, or in any work-related setting.

### 11.3 Reporting Harassment

- Workforce members who experience or witness harassment shall report it immediately to their manager, HR, or the [PLACEHOLDER: ethics/compliance hotline].
- All harassment reports shall be investigated promptly and confidentially.
- Appropriate corrective action shall be taken against individuals found to have engaged in harassment.

## 12. Compliance with Laws

### 12.1 Legal Compliance

All workforce members shall comply with applicable federal, state, and local laws and regulations, including but not limited to:

- HIPAA Privacy, Security, and Breach Notification Rules.
- Anti-kickback statutes and Stark Law provisions relevant to healthcare consulting.
- False Claims Act.
- Anti-bribery and anti-corruption laws (including the Foreign Corrupt Practices Act, if applicable).
- Employment and labor laws.
- Tax laws and regulations.
- Intellectual property laws.
- Export control regulations.

### 12.2 Regulatory Obligations

- Workforce members shall cooperate with government investigations and audits.
- Do not destroy, alter, or conceal documents or records that may be relevant to a government investigation or legal proceeding.
- Report any government inquiries or subpoenas to [PLACEHOLDER: legal contact] immediately.

## 13. Enforcement and Disciplinary Action

### 13.1 Violations

Violations of this Code of Conduct may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment or engagement. The severity of the disciplinary action shall be commensurate with the nature and severity of the violation.

### 13.2 Disciplinary Actions

Disciplinary actions may include, but are not limited to:

| Severity | Potential Actions |
|----------|------------------|
| Minor | Verbal counseling, additional training |
| Moderate | Written warning, performance improvement plan, enhanced monitoring |
| Serious | Suspension, demotion, reassignment, removal from engagement |
| Severe | Immediate termination, referral to law enforcement |

### 13.3 Factors Considered

The following factors shall be considered in determining disciplinary action:

- Severity and impact of the violation.
- Whether the violation was intentional or unintentional.
- Whether there is a pattern of violations.
- Whether the workforce member self-reported the violation.
- Whether the workforce member cooperated with the investigation.
- The workforce member's employment history and prior disciplinary record.
