# HIPAA Security Rule Compliance Policy

## 1. Purpose

The purpose of this policy is to establish Pivot Point Consulting's requirements for compliance with the HIPAA Security Rule (45 CFR Part 160 and Subparts A and C of Part 164). This policy defines the administrative, physical, and technical safeguards that Pivot Point Consulting implements to ensure the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of electronic Protected Health Information (ePHI) that the organization creates, receives, maintains, or transmits on behalf of Covered Entity clients.

As a healthcare IT consulting firm operating as a Business Associate, Pivot Point Consulting recognizes the critical importance of maintaining robust security controls to protect ePHI across all consulting engagements, internal systems, and third-party interactions.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to:

- All electronic Protected Health Information (ePHI) that Pivot Point Consulting creates, receives, maintains, or transmits.
- All information systems, applications, networks, and infrastructure components that store, process, or transmit ePHI.
- All workforce members, including employees, contractors, consultants, interns, and temporary staff who may access ePHI.
- All physical locations, remote work environments, and cloud environments used to handle ePHI.
- All third parties, including subcontractors and vendors, with access to Pivot Point Consulting systems or ePHI.

## 3. Definitions

### 3.1 Electronic Protected Health Information (ePHI)

Protected Health Information that is created, received, maintained, or transmitted in electronic form. This includes ePHI stored on servers, workstations, mobile devices, removable media, and in cloud environments.

### 3.2 Administrative Safeguards

Administrative actions, policies, and procedures to manage the selection, development, implementation, and maintenance of security measures to protect ePHI and to manage the conduct of the workforce in relation to the protection of that information.

### 3.3 Physical Safeguards

Physical measures, policies, and procedures to protect information systems and related buildings and equipment from natural and environmental hazards and unauthorized intrusion.

### 3.4 Technical Safeguards

The technology and the policy and procedures for its use that protect ePHI and control access to it.

### 3.5 Security Incident

The attempted or successful unauthorized access, use, disclosure, modification, or destruction of information or interference with system operations in an information system.

### 3.6 Risk Analysis

An accurate and thorough assessment of the potential risks and vulnerabilities to the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of ePHI held by the organization.

## 4. Administrative Safeguards

### 4.1 Security Management Process (Required - 45 CFR 164.308(a)(1))

Pivot Point Consulting shall implement policies and procedures to prevent, detect, contain, and correct security violations. This includes:

#### 4.1.1 Risk Analysis

- Conduct a comprehensive risk analysis at least annually, and whenever there is a significant change to the organization's environment, operations, or technology infrastructure.
- Document identified risks, including the likelihood and impact of potential threats to ePHI.
- Use a standardized risk assessment methodology aligned with NIST SP 800-30 or equivalent framework.
- Maintain risk analysis documentation for a minimum of six (6) years.

#### 4.1.2 Risk Management

- Implement security measures sufficient to reduce risks and vulnerabilities to a reasonable and appropriate level.
- Develop and maintain a risk register documenting identified risks, risk ratings, mitigation strategies, and responsible parties.
- Review and update the risk register at least quarterly.

#### 4.1.3 Sanction Policy

- Apply appropriate sanctions against workforce members who fail to comply with security policies and procedures.
- Sanctions shall be commensurate with the severity of the violation and applied consistently.
- Document all sanctions and maintain records for a minimum of six (6) years.

#### 4.1.4 Information System Activity Review

- Implement procedures to regularly review records of information system activity, such as audit logs, access reports, and security incident tracking reports.

## 6. Technical Safeguards

### 6.1 Access Control (Required - 45 CFR 164.312(a))

#### 6.1.1 Unique User Identification

- Assign a unique name and/or number for identifying and tracking user identity in all systems containing ePHI.
- Prohibit the use of shared or group accounts for accessing ePHI.
- Service accounts accessing ePHI shall be documented, approved, and monitored.

#### 6.1.2 Emergency Access Procedure

- Establish procedures for obtaining necessary ePHI during an emergency.
- Define emergency access roles and approval procedures.
- Log and review all emergency access usage within [PLACEHOLDER: number of hours] hours.

#### 6.1.3 Automatic Logoff

- Implement electronic procedures that terminate an electronic session after a predetermined time of inactivity.
- Sessions accessing ePHI shall time out after [PLACEHOLDER: number] minutes of inactivity.

#### 6.1.4 Encryption and Decryption

- Implement a mechanism to encrypt and decrypt ePHI at rest and in transit.
- Encryption standards: AES-256 or equivalent for data at rest; TLS 1.2 or higher for data in transit.
- Encryption keys shall be managed in accordance with [PLACEHOLDER: key management policy reference].

### 6.2 Audit Controls (Required - 45 CFR 164.312(b))

- Implement hardware, software, and procedural mechanisms that record and examine activity in information systems that contain or use ePHI.
- Audit logs shall capture, at minimum: user identification, date/time, type of event, success/failure, and data or resource affected.
- Audit logs shall be retained for a minimum of [PLACEHOLDER: retention period, e.g., six years].
- Audit logs shall be protected from unauthorized modification or deletion.
- Automated alerting shall be configured for suspicious activities, including: failed login attempts, access outside normal hours, bulk data downloads, and privilege escalation.

### 6.3 Integrity Controls (Required - 45 CFR 164.312(c))

#### 6.3.1 Mechanism to Authenticate ePHI

- Implement electronic mechanisms to corroborate that ePHI has not been altered or destroyed in an unauthorized manner.
- Use checksums, digital signatures, or equivalent mechanisms to verify data integrity.
- Monitor databases and file systems containing ePHI for unauthorized changes using file integrity monitoring (FIM) tools.

### 6.4 Person or Entity Authentication (Required - 45 CFR 164.312(d))

- Implement procedures to verify that a person or entity seeking access to ePHI is the one claimed.
- Multi-factor authentication (MFA) is required for all remote access to systems containing ePHI.
- MFA is required for all administrative access to systems containing ePHI.
- Authentication mechanisms shall include at least two of: something you know, something you have, something you are.

### 6.5 Transmission Security (Required - 45 CFR 164.312(e))

#### 6.5.1 Integrity Controls

- Implement security measures to ensure that electronically transmitted ePHI is not improperly modified without detection until disposed of.

#### 6.5.2 Encryption

- Implement a mechanism to encrypt ePHI whenever deemed appropriate during transmission.
- All ePHI transmissions over public networks shall be encrypted using TLS 1.2 or higher.
- Email containing ePHI shall use approved encryption methods ([PLACEHOLDER: specific email encryption solution]).
- VPN connections shall use [PLACEHOLDER: VPN encryption standard, e.g., IPSec, WireGuard] encryption.

## 7. Organizational Requirements

### 7.1 Business Associate Contracts

- Pivot Point Consulting shall ensure that Business Associate Agreements are in place with all Business Associates and subcontractors that create, receive, maintain, or transmit ePHI on its behalf.
- BAAs shall include the required provisions as specified in the BAA Management Policy.

### 7.2 Policies and Procedures

- Implement reasonable and appropriate policies and procedures to comply with the standards, implementation specifications, and other requirements of the Security Rule.
- Retain documentation for six (6) years from the date of creation or the date when it last was in effect, whichever is later.
- Review and update policies and procedures at least annually.

### 7.3 Documentation

- Maintain written (which may be electronic) documentation of all Security Rule-required policies, procedures, actions, activities, and assessments.
- Make documentation available to workforce members responsible for implementing applicable procedures.
- Update documentation periodically in response to environmental or organizational changes.

## 8. Roles and Responsibilities

### 8.1 Security Officer

The Security Officer ([PLACEHOLDER: Name/Title]) is responsible for:

- Overseeing the implementation and maintenance of Security Rule safeguards.
- Conducting and coordinating risk analyses and risk management activities.
- Managing security incident response and investigation.
- Coordinating security awareness training programs.
- Reporting security compliance status to [PLACEHOLDER: executive title/committee].
- Ensuring evaluation of security controls on a regular basis.

### 8.2 IT Operations

IT Operations is responsible for:

- Implementing and maintaining technical security controls.
- Managing access control systems and user provisioning/deprovisioning.
- Monitoring system activity, audit logs, and security alerts.
- Maintaining backup and disaster recovery systems.
- Applying patches and updates to systems containing ePHI.
- Managing endpoint protection and network security tools.

### 8.3 Engagement Managers

Engagement Managers are responsible for:

- Ensuring that security requirements are addressed in engagement planning.
- Verifying that workforce members assigned to engagements have completed required security training.
- Monitoring compliance with security policies during engagements.
- Reporting security incidents to the Security Officer.

### 8.4 Workforce Members

All workforce members are responsible for:

- Complying with all security policies and procedures.
- Completing required security training.
- Reporting security incidents and vulnerabilities.
- Protecting their authentication credentials.
- Securing workstations and devices.
