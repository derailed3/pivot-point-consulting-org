# HIPAA Privacy Rule Compliance Policy

## 1. Purpose

The purpose of this policy is to establish Pivot Point Consulting's requirements for compliance with the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA) Privacy Rule (45 CFR Part 160 and Subparts A and E of Part 164). As a healthcare IT consulting organization that routinely operates as a Business Associate to Covered Entities, Pivot Point Consulting is committed to protecting the privacy of Protected Health Information (PHI) in all consulting engagements, internal operations, and third-party interactions.

This policy ensures that all workforce members understand their obligations under the HIPAA Privacy Rule and that organizational processes are designed to safeguard patient privacy throughout the consulting lifecycle.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to:

- All Pivot Point Consulting workforce members, including full-time employees, part-time employees, contractors, interns, and temporary staff.
- All consulting engagements where Pivot Point Consulting may create, receive, maintain, or transmit PHI on behalf of Covered Entity clients.
- All departments, divisions, and operating units of Pivot Point Consulting.
- All physical locations, remote work environments, and virtual workspaces used for Pivot Point Consulting business.
- All systems, applications, and media that create, store, process, or transmit PHI.

## 3. Definitions

### 3.1 Protected Health Information (PHI)

Individually identifiable health information transmitted or maintained in any form or medium (electronic, paper, or oral) that is created or received by a healthcare provider, health plan, employer, or healthcare clearinghouse and relates to the past, present, or future physical or mental health condition of an individual, the provision of healthcare to an individual, or the past, present, or future payment for the provision of healthcare to an individual.

### 3.2 Covered Entity

A health plan, healthcare clearinghouse, or healthcare provider that transmits any health information in electronic form in connection with a HIPAA-covered transaction. Pivot Point Consulting's clients are typically Covered Entities.

### 3.3 Business Associate

A person or entity that performs certain functions or activities on behalf of, or provides certain services to, a Covered Entity that involve the use or disclosure of PHI. Pivot Point Consulting operates as a Business Associate to its Covered Entity clients.

### 3.4 Minimum Necessary Standard

The principle that, to the extent practical, only the minimum amount of PHI necessary to accomplish the intended purpose of the use, disclosure, or request should be used or disclosed. This standard applies to all uses and disclosures except treatment purposes, disclosures to the individual, and disclosures required by law.

### 3.5 Notice of Privacy Practices (NPP)

A document that Covered Entities must provide to individuals describing the ways in which the entity may use and disclose PHI and informing individuals of their rights with respect to their PHI.

### 3.6 Designated Record Set

A group of records maintained by or for a Covered Entity that includes medical records, billing records, enrollment records, payment records, claims adjudication records, and other records used to make decisions about individuals.

## 4. Policy Statement

### 4.1 Use and Disclosure of PHI

Pivot Point Consulting shall use and disclose PHI only as permitted or required by the HIPAA Privacy Rule and as specified in applicable Business Associate Agreements (BAAs). All uses and disclosures of PHI shall be:

- Limited to the minimum necessary to accomplish the intended purpose.
- Consistent with the terms of the applicable BAA.
- Documented and tracked through approved systems.
- Subject to the authorization requirements described in this policy.

Workforce members shall not access, use, or disclose PHI unless they have a legitimate business need and authorization to do so within the scope of their role and the applicable engagement.

### 4.2 Minimum Necessary Standard

Pivot Point Consulting applies the minimum necessary standard to all uses and disclosures of PHI. Implementation includes:

- **Role-Based Access**: Access to PHI is limited to workforce members whose roles require such access for a specific consulting engagement.
- **Engagement-Specific Access**: PHI access is scoped to the specific client engagement and terminated upon engagement completion.
- **Data Limitation**: When PHI is required for analysis, testing, or development purposes, de-identified or limited data sets shall be used whenever possible.
- **Request Limitation**: When requesting PHI from clients or other entities, Pivot Point Consulting shall request only the minimum amount of PHI necessary to fulfill the engagement objectives.

### 4.4 Notice of Privacy Practices

While Pivot Point Consulting is not required to maintain its own Notice of Privacy Practices as a Business Associate, the organization shall:

- Assist Covered Entity clients in developing and maintaining compliant NPPs when such services are within the engagement scope.
- Ensure that workforce members understand the NPP requirements imposed on Covered Entity clients.
- Not engage in any use or disclosure of PHI that would be inconsistent with the client's NPP.

## 5. Individual Rights

Pivot Point Consulting shall support Covered Entity clients in honoring the following individual rights under the HIPAA Privacy Rule.

### 5.1 Right of Access

- Individuals have the right to access, inspect, and obtain a copy of their PHI in a Designated Record Set.
- Pivot Point Consulting shall assist clients in providing access within 30 days of receipt of a request (with one 30-day extension if necessary).
- When Pivot Point Consulting maintains PHI in electronic format on behalf of a client, the organization shall be prepared to provide PHI in the electronic format requested by the individual, if readily producible.

### 5.2 Right to Amendment

- Individuals have the right to request amendments to their PHI in a Designated Record Set.
- Pivot Point Consulting shall forward any amendment requests received directly to the applicable Covered Entity client within [PLACEHOLDER: number of business days] business days.
- The organization shall implement approved amendments in systems under its control within [PLACEHOLDER: number of business days] business days of receiving direction from the Covered Entity client.

### 5.3 Right to an Accounting of Disclosures

- Individuals have the right to receive an accounting of certain disclosures of their PHI made in the six years prior to the request.
- Pivot Point Consulting shall maintain records of disclosures that are subject to accounting requirements.
- Disclosure records shall include: date of disclosure, name and address of the recipient, description of the PHI disclosed, and the purpose of the disclosure.

### 5.4 Right to Request Restrictions

- Individuals have the right to request restrictions on certain uses and disclosures of their PHI.
- Pivot Point Consulting shall forward any restriction requests received directly to the applicable Covered Entity client within [PLACEHOLDER: number of business days] business days.
- If a restriction is agreed to by the Covered Entity client, Pivot Point Consulting shall implement the restriction in all systems and processes under its control.

### 5.5 Right to Confidential Communications

- Individuals have the right to request that communications containing PHI be made by alternative means or at alternative locations.
- Pivot Point Consulting shall comply with reasonable requests from Covered Entity clients to accommodate such individual requests.

### 5.6 Right to a Paper Copy of the Notice of Privacy Practices

- Pivot Point Consulting shall support Covered Entity clients in providing paper copies of their NPP upon request.

## 6. Uses and Disclosures

### 6.1 Treatment, Payment, and Healthcare Operations

As a Business Associate, Pivot Point Consulting may use or disclose PHI as necessary to perform services for Covered Entity clients in the following categories:

- **Treatment**: Supporting client systems used for treatment purposes, including EHR implementations, clinical workflow optimization, and health information exchange.
- **Payment**: Supporting client systems used for billing, claims processing, and revenue cycle management.
- **Healthcare Operations**: Supporting quality improvement, compliance, auditing, business planning, and other operational activities of Covered Entity clients.

All such uses and disclosures must be within the scope of the applicable BAA.

### 6.2 Authorization Requirements

Uses and disclosures of PHI not otherwise permitted by the Privacy Rule require a valid written authorization from the individual. Pivot Point Consulting shall:

- Not use or disclose PHI for purposes that require individual authorization unless a valid authorization has been obtained by the Covered Entity client.
- Verify that appropriate authorizations exist before using PHI for marketing, sale of PHI, or other purposes requiring authorization.
- Not condition services on the individual providing an authorization.

### 6.3 Permitted Disclosures Without Authorization

Pivot Point Consulting recognizes the following categories of disclosures permitted without individual authorization, subject to applicable BAA terms:

- Disclosures required by law.
- Disclosures for public health activities.
- Disclosures about victims of abuse, neglect, or domestic violence.
- Disclosures for health oversight activities.
- Disclosures for judicial and administrative proceedings.
- Disclosures for law enforcement purposes.
- Disclosures to avert a serious threat to health or safety.
- Disclosures for specialized government functions.
- Disclosures for workers' compensation.

Any such disclosure must be coordinated with and approved by the applicable Covered Entity client and Pivot Point Consulting's Privacy Officer.

### 6.4 De-Identified Information

Information that has been de-identified in accordance with 45 CFR 164.514(a)-(c) is not considered PHI and is not subject to the Privacy Rule. Pivot Point Consulting may de-identify PHI using:

- **Safe Harbor Method**: Removal of the 18 specified identifiers and confirmation that the remaining information cannot be used to identify an individual.
- **Expert Determination Method**: Application of statistical or scientific principles by a qualified expert to determine that the risk of identifying an individual is very small.

## 8. Training Requirements

### 8.1 Initial Training

All workforce members shall complete HIPAA Privacy Rule training within [PLACEHOLDER: number of days] days of hire or engagement start date. Training shall cover:

- Overview of HIPAA Privacy Rule requirements.
- Pivot Point Consulting's privacy policies and procedures.
- Individual rights under the Privacy Rule.
- Minimum necessary standard.
- Incident reporting procedures.
- Sanctions for non-compliance.

### 8.2 Annual Refresher Training

All workforce members shall complete annual HIPAA Privacy Rule refresher training. Refresher training shall include updates to regulations, policies, and lessons learned from privacy incidents.

### 8.3 Engagement-Specific Training

Workforce members assigned to consulting engagements involving PHI shall complete engagement-specific privacy training that covers:

- Client-specific privacy requirements and policies.
- Scope of permitted PHI access for the engagement.
- Client-specific incident reporting procedures.
- Any restrictions or special requirements defined in the BAA.

### 8.4 Training Documentation

All privacy training shall be documented, including the date of training, content covered, attendees, and trainer. Training records shall be maintained for a minimum of six (6) years.

## 9. Sanctions

### 9.1 Violation Categories

Privacy Rule violations are categorized as follows:

| Category | Description | Example |
|----------|-------------|---------|
| Level 1 - Minor | Unintentional violation with no harm | Sending an internal email with minimal PHI to an authorized recipient without encryption |
| Level 2 - Moderate | Negligent violation or pattern of minor violations | Failing to log off a client system containing PHI when unattended |
| Level 3 - Serious | Knowing violation or significant harm | Accessing PHI without a legitimate business need |
| Level 4 - Severe | Willful neglect or malicious intent | Intentional unauthorized disclosure of PHI |

### 9.2 Sanctions by Category

| Category | Potential Sanctions |
|----------|-------------------|
| Level 1 | Verbal warning, additional training |
| Level 2 | Written warning, additional training, enhanced monitoring |
| Level 3 | Suspension, termination, removal from engagement |
| Level 4 | Immediate termination, referral to law enforcement if applicable |

### 9.3 Mitigating Factors

Mitigating factors that may be considered include self-reporting, cooperation with investigation, and absence of prior violations. Aggravating factors include prior violations, failure to report, and attempt to conceal the violation.
